Yuan "Larry" Liu

PhD student
University of Wisconsin-Madison

I am a PhD student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, advised by Dr. Daniel Wright. My research focuses on advancing the understanding and prediction of extreme precipitation and flood hazards under climate change. On the methodology side, I developed advanced statistic methods for precipitation stochastic downscaling and rainfall/flood frequency analysis. On the application side, I investigated mechanisms and risks of extreme precipitation and floods under climate change, with a particular focus on the Mississippi River Basin.

Before coming to UW-Madison, I received a bachelor's degree in Harbor, Waterway, and Coastal Engineering from Dalian University of Technology, advised by Dr. Chongwei Zhang.
